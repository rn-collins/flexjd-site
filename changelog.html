What's New

A running log of real updates to this site

No filler entries. Every line below reflects an actual change that shipped — new opportunity listings, fixed bugs, new tools. If you're wondering whether something changed recently, check here before assuming.

Update Log

Jul 22, 2026
Three more verified remote/part-time additions. Kept crawling for new listings and found three that hold up under the same "does it show a real, current, applyable position" standard: Disability Rights New York's Fall 2026 Legal Intern role (fully remote across its Albany, Brooklyn, and Rochester offices, full- or part-time, rolling admission, open to any year); the Government Accountability Project's Fall 2026 Legal Research Intern position (remote or hybrid DC, candidate's choice, flexible 30-40 hrs/week, apply by Jul 31); and Public Citizen's Protect Democracy Internship (fully remote, nationwide team, some paid slots at $17.95/hr, up to 40 hrs/week for about 8 weeks, paid-track deadline Jul 31). Passed over several others that didn't clear the bar: Society for the Rule of Law (position filled), CIEL (2026 cycle closed, next opens September), EFF (requires 1-2 in-office days, not remote), Earthjustice's DC Fall 2026 role (May 15 deadline already passed), SPLC's Summer 2026 program (deadline passed in February), National Health Law Program (program already underway), Free Speech For People (window likely lapsed), and the DNC Voter Protection Internship (June 12 deadline passed). Tracker now stands at 51 remote/part-time listings, 369 verified opportunities site-wide.
Jul 22, 2026
Extended the precise audit past opportunities.html — every external citation link, checked individually. Pulled all 225 external (non-Northeastern) links across resources.html, newsletter.html, and all 7 campaign pages and checked each one against a standard suited to what it actually is: these are informational citations and "learn more" resources backing up factual claims in awareness content, not application pages, so the bar was "does this resolve to real, live, on-topic content," not "is there an apply button." Caught and fixed two syntax bugs first — a trailing period baked into the mass.gov human-trafficking link and the OHCHR universal-declaration link, both of which broke the URL outright. Prioritized the 62 deep-path links (the ones most likely to have rotted) for individual verification over the ~160 plain organization homepages (ACLU, RAINN, NAACP LDF, etc. — stable, well-known domains, low risk). Found and fixed 6 more: the ABA's women's-issues page had moved under its Diversity Center restructuring; the Boston Bar Association renamed its Young Lawyers Division to the New Lawyers Network and the old URL is dead; two Mass.gov shortlinks (Attorney General's Civil Rights Division, and the driver's-license gender-marker change page) no longer resolve at their old slugs; Northeastern's own sexual-assault-awareness resource actually lives on the library's subject-guides subdomain, not neu.edu directly; and the NIH/PMC citation was pointing at nih.gov instead of the actual PMC article host. All six repointed to their correct, verified current URLs. The remaining 217 links — including all four internal Qualtrics survey forms and every major-organization homepage — checked out as real and live. All div/tr structure verified balanced across every touched file.
Jul 22, 2026
Precise, one-by-one audit of the external scholarship table — the real thing, not a spot-check. Went through all 31 links in the "Foundation-Based Scholarships" and "Additional Scholarships and Grants" tables individually, checking whether each destination actually shows a real, specific, applyable scholarship page — not just whether it resolves. Fixed 16 links that were stale or pointed to the wrong page: AccessLex's Scholarship Databank had moved to a new path; Boston Bar Foundation's link went to a generic about page while its actual Gants Judicial Scholarship lives on massbarfoundation.org; HNBF's own domain (hnbf.org) turned out to be a dead site frozen since 2015 — the real program is HNBA's VIA Fund at hnba.com; APABA's educational-fund domain doesn't resolve at all, and the current org is the NAPABA Law Foundation; the National LGBT Bar Foundation is now the National LGBTQ+ Bar, with a working scholarships page instead of a bare "about" link. Repointed NAWL, AILA, NACDL Foundation, the New York Bar Foundation, California ChangeLawyers, Phi Delta Phi, the South Asian Bar Association, the Federal Bar Association (scholarships actually run through its separate Foundation), the Hispanic Scholarship Fund, the ABA Tax Section's Brunswick Fellowship, and the Federalist Society's Olin/Searle/Smith Fellowship from bare homepages to their actual program pages. Removed 7 listings that didn't hold up under the standard: National Bar Association (no locatable scholarship page on their site at all), NELA (no dedicated law-student scholarship program exists), AAPD's "Justice for All Scholarship" (couldn't confirm this program still exists under that name), the Jack Kent Cooke Graduate Arts Award (fine arts and creative writing only — not law-relevant, shouldn't have been listed), the ABA Labor & Employment Law diversity scholarship (no specific page found), Texas Bar Foundation (funds nonprofits only, not individual student scholarships — a category mismatch), and the ACS Law Student Chapters listing (a membership program, not a scholarship). Left Soros, ABA's Legal Opportunity Scholarship, the Massachusetts Bar Foundation's Legal Intern Fellowship, NAACP LDF's Earl Warren Scholars, Point Foundation, Pat Tillman Foundation, and Illinois Bar Foundation as-is — all real, structured, recurring annual programs, even where this year's specific window has already closed. Table now stands at 27 carefully verified scholarship links (down from 34), bringing the site total to 366 verified listings.
Jul 22, 2026
Second-pass Opportunity Tracker audit — link destinations, not just link validity. Re-checked every Remote & Part-Time Clerkships listing against a stricter bar: does the destination page show an actual current, specific position to apply to, not just a resolving, topically-relevant page. Removed 7 more entries whose links loaded fine but led to generic careers pages with no real listing (Immigration Equality, VoteRiders, Center for Biological Diversity, Human Rights First, Center for Reproductive Rights, Center for Appellate Litigation, and Animal Legal Defense Fund). Combined with the earlier pass, that's 12 entries removed from 383. Re-checked FIRE, Institute for Justice, NY Attorney General, and SELC against the same standard — all four held up, since their listings honestly describe ongoing rolling programs rather than claiming a single dead-end posting. Sourced and verified three solid replacements: Liberty Justice Center's Fall 2026 remote internship ($20/hr, apply by Aug 15), Amica Center for Immigrant Rights' fully remote Fall 2026 Immigration Impact Lab internship (apply by Jul 31), and Pennsylvania Institutional Law Project's remote-eligible Fall 2026 semester externship — bringing the tracker to 372. Continued the audit through more of the list: NCLR, NCLC, GLAD Law, and Privacy Rights Clearinghouse all held up (real, current, specific programs), but Farmers Insurance's linked job posting had expired with no Law Clerk role among its current openings, and HRC's internship program turned out to be closed to applications for every 2026 term. Removed both, landing at 370 verified opportunities. Also fixed a display bug where long URLs in the Link column wrapped character-by-character, and trimmed overlong Notes cells to their key fact. Finished the full pass through every remaining Remote & Part-Time Clerkships row: Allstate, Shute Mihaly & Weinberger, ACLU of Missouri, Environmental Integrity Project, EEOC, Illinois Attorney General, NIPNLG, Massachusetts AG's Office, LWV, AFSA, The Lawyering Project, The Commons Law Center, ELC-PA, GAGV, PLAN, Cohen Weiss and Simon, Wikimedia Foundation, AWI, Animal Outlook, Juvenile Law Center, NDRC, NCUIH, FPF, Education Law Center, CAIDP, The Leadership Conference on Civil and Human Rights, Colorado Department of Law, and Center for Justice and Accountability all held up under the strict standard — real, current, specific programs, most on rolling admission. The one exception: CDC's Public Health Law Program page still described a 2025 cohort whose application window and appointment had already closed, with no current cycle open. Removed it, landing the tracker at a fully audited 369 verified opportunities — every single listing now checked against the "does it show a real, applyable position" bar. Kept crawling for new remote/part-time listings and found two that check out: the Center for Constitutional Rights' Fall 2026 legal internship (remote, 12-20 hrs/week, paid $2,200-$7,500, apply by Aug 7) and Justice in Motion's Law Student Internship supporting its migrant-rights Defender Network (remote, ~35 hrs/week, live posting on their careers board). Passed over the Center for International Environmental Law's Fall 2026 internship — a real program, but its stated application deadline of June 15, 2026 has already passed with no new cycle open yet — and Society for the Rule of Law's internship, which is marked filled. Tracker now stands at 371 verified opportunities.
Jul 22, 2026
Site-wide link audit — beyond the tracker. Prompted by a spot-check of the NUSL funding table, ran a full sweep of every clickable link across the site (opportunities.html's two funding tables, resources.html, newsletter.html, all 7 campaign pages, glossary, and class-year pages) for a bug deeper than "is it dead": nearly 90 links pointed to bare northeastern.edu instead of the correct subdomain (law.northeastern.edu, uhcs.northeastern.edu, ouec.northeastern.edu, lgbtqa.northeastern.edu, cssh.northeastern.edu, gsg.northeastern.edu), so they loaded a blank or wrong page instead of the scholarship, clinic, or center they claimed to link to. Repointed every one to its correct subdomain and verified a sample against live content — CLEAR, the Health Policy and Law Scholarship, CLIC, the Domestic Violence Clinic, and CGLJ all check out. Caught one live rename in the process: the "Institute on Race and Justice (IRJ)" has become the Center on Crime, Race, and Justice (CRJ) — updated the entry name, link, and description accordingly, and flagged that its co-directorship affiliation needs re-verification under the new name. Converted several broken pseudo-links (an office name glued to a link showing only the bare domain) into working mailto: addresses. Fixed a duplicate broken contact button and a garbled sentence fragment on the CGLJ card in the December campaign page. All div/tr structure verified balanced across every touched file.
