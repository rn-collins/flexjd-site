What's New

A running log of real updates to this site

Update Log

Jul 23, 2026
A deeper editorial and engineering review closed a real security gap, fixed a factual error, and completed the sitemap. Also flagged (but hasn't yet fixed) that these changelog entries themselves are too long and dense — you're looking at the fix.
See what changedBrought in six specialist passes (copyediting, copywriting critique, fact-checking, code quality, architecture/security, and UX) to look past link accuracy at the site's actual writing, code, and structure. Fixed what was safe and unambiguous: a security gap where internal working documents (campaign drafts, the opportunity tracker spreadsheet) and 26 one-off build scripts had no deploy-exclusion rule and were likely publicly downloadable — added a proper ignore rule. Completed the sitemap, which was missing the glossary, this changelog, and all four class-year pages. Fixed a factual math error (the UDHR's age was stated as 76 years in three spots when it's actually 78). Made about 50 small copyediting fixes — inconsistent date/time formatting, missing punctuation, a few typos — with no change to meaning anywhere. Consolidated three pages' worth of byte-for-byte duplicated search-bar CSS into one shared location in style.css.

Left unapplied, and reported instead, because they need a judgment call rather than a mechanical fix: a copywriting critique flagging that changelog entries here run too long and dense for a student audience (fair, and something to act on); several stale deadlines in the opportunities table that need a fresh sweep now that mid-to-late-July postings have closed; a few internal date inconsistencies in the Student Guide's academic calendar that need reconciling to one source of truth; and a UX finding that class-year pages aren't reachable from the main navigation. Those are next.
Jul 22, 2026
Fixed 14 broken or misdirected links sitewide, including one that had been silently redirecting Northeastern's own DVI Legal Clinic citation to an unrelated gambling site. Every external link on the site has now been checked individually, not sampled.
See what changedThe earlier link audit round verified all deep-path links but treated roughly 160 plain organization-homepage citations as low-risk without checking each one. Went back and checked every one of those, plus glossary.html, index.html, and the four class-year pages, individually via search and direct fetch. Found 14 real problems, including a genuinely concerning one: the link cited for Northeastern's own DVI Legal Clinic, dvilegal.org, has been squatted and now resolves to an unrelated Australian online-gambling affiliate site — repointed it to the clinic's real page on law.northeastern.edu. Also caught: alphaphialpha.com resolved to a blank page (the real Alpha Phi Alpha site is apa1906.net); the AFSP donor page needed its actual donordrive subdomain; "End Rape on Campus" was linked to the wrong bare domain (campus.org) instead of endrapeoncampus.org; three Massachusetts-area orgs (Boston Fair Housing, LIFT Worcester, Mass Fair Housing Center) had slightly-off domain spellings pointing nowhere; NCADV's old domain now just redirects to a merger notice, so both of its citations now point to the National DV Hotline directly; National Survivor Alliance didn't resolve under that name and is now pointed to the National Survivor Network; a Northeastern database-tutorials link and a "Northeastern afford.com" financial-aid tool link were both on the wrong domain entirely; Greater Boston's PFLAG chapter had a dead old URL; and the Transgender Emergency Fund citation was pointing at a blank, unconfigured lookalike domain instead of the real one. The remaining 154 checked links were all confirmed real, live, and on-topic — including the Skadden Fellowship application pages, which show a genuinely open Class of 2027 cycle (deadline Aug 31, 2026). Every external link on the site — informational citations and the opportunity/scholarship trackers alike — has now been checked individually, not sampled. All div/tr structure verified balanced across every touched file.
Jul 22, 2026
Added 4 more verified remote/part-time listings and fixed a batch of accessibility and mobile-layout bugs, including a horizontal-scroll issue on phones. Tracker now stands at 55 remote/part-time listings, 373 verified opportunities site-wide.
See what changedKept crawling and found four more listings that hold up: Texas Immigration Law Council's Fall 2026 Legal Intern role (remote, $15/hr or credit, apply by Jul 30); two Brennan Center for Justice Fall 2026 internships — Democracy Legal Internship and Liberty and National Security Legal Internship — both part-time at $20.90/hr with a Jul 24 deadline; and Consumer Watchdog's rolling-admission Volunteer Law Student Intern role (hybrid or fully remote). Passed on several that looked promising but didn't clear the bar: Earthjustice and ALDF's externships had deadlines that already passed, Society for the Rule of Law's role is filled, several ACLU "internship" listings turned out to be dead scrapes on third-party aggregators with zero matching current openings on ACLU's own site, and a handful of others were stale, closed, or lacked a real part-time/remote component. Tracker now stands at 55 remote/part-time listings, 373 verified opportunities site-wide.

Alongside that, ran a genuine accessibility and visual-QA pass across every page rather than a cosmetic one: fixed a real heading-hierarchy bug where footer section labels were inconsistently marked as h3 on some pages and h4 on others (an actual skipped heading level on the class-year pages), added aria-current="page" to every active nav link so assistive tech gets the same signal sighted users get from the underline, and caught a knock-on CSS bug where the footer heading style only targeted h4 — five pages were silently rendering unstyled footer labels until the selector was widened. On the visual side, fixed a mobile overflow bug in the `.frames` card grid (its minimum column width was 3px too wide for a 375px screen, forcing horizontal scroll) and bumped the opportunity-tracker's filter chips from a ~33px tap target up to a proper ~40px+ to make them easier to hit on mobile. Verified every table, nav pattern, focus state, and touch target already met the bar and left those alone rather than "fixing" things that weren't broken. All div/tr structure and CSS brace balance verified across every touched file.
