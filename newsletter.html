Public Interest Fellowships — Summer Planning Guide

Skadden Fellowship — deadline 11:59 p.m. PT, August 31, 2026. Open to students in the fall of their final year, LLM candidates, clerks, or graduates within the prior two years. Fully funds two years of public-interest legal work at a host organization you help identify. Source: skaddenfellowships.org/applicant.

Equal Justice Works Fellowship — deadline September 17, 2026 (Design-Your-Own track). Same model: you propose a project and partner organization. Source: equaljusticeworks.org/fellowships-opportunities.

Do this over the summer, not in August: both fellowships are won on the strength of a specific, well-scoped project proposal and a host organization willing to sponsor you. Start those conversations now — a rushed proposal in the final weeks rarely beats one built over a full summer. NUSL's Public Interest Law Support office and CPIAC can help you shape a proposal: law.northeastern.edu/academics/centers/cpiac.

Federal Student Loans — Major Changes Effective July 1, 2026

⚠️ The One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA) eliminates the Grad PLUS loan program for new borrowers starting July 1, 2026. To partially offset that, the Direct Unsubsidized Stafford annual cap for professional students (JD included) rises from $20,500 to $50,000/year, with a $200,000 lifetime cap on graduate/professional borrowing (part of a $257,500 all-in lifetime federal loan limit across all levels).

If you already borrowed Grad PLUS for your current program before July 1, 2026 and continue in that same program into 2026–27, you can keep borrowing Unsubsidized and Grad PLUS at your current limits — this change mainly hits new borrowers and anyone starting a new program after the cutoff.

Also eliminated for loans taken on or after July 1, 2026: current income-driven repayment (IDR) plans, plus extended and graduated repayment plans. Talk to Financial Aid before assuming your existing repayment strategy still applies to any new borrowing. Source: studentfinance.northeastern.edu/applying-for-aid/federal-updates; background: ABA Journal, OBBBA loan changes.

Note: these are new borrowing caps under the incoming structure. Separately, confirmed interest rates for loans first disbursed July 1, 2026–June 30, 2027 are 8.07% (Direct Unsubsidized Stafford, graduate) and 9.07% (Grad PLUS, legacy borrowers only) — up from 7.94%/8.94% in 2025–26. Source: fsapartners.ed.gov, Federal Register interest rate notice. The rate table and the borrowing-cap figures above answer different questions (what your loans cost vs. how much you can borrow going forward) — don't conflate them.

This is not financial advice. Loan terms, interest rates, and eligibility change; confirm your specific numbers with Northeastern's Office of Student Financial Services before making any borrowing decision.

Career Timing: What Every FlexJD Student Should Know Now

Adapted from a practitioner mentorship conversation
transactional attorney, M&A and dispute resolution, 20+ years experience. Career positioning in law starts earlier than most students expect. Here is what to do, by cohort.

Questions on career strategy? Contact Hanna Kim in NUSL Career Placement or email the SBA Chair at collins.ra@northeastern.edu.

1LsBig law now recruits 2L summer positions during 1L year — do not wait until September to begin outreach. On-ground JD peers are typically applying with fall grades in hand by October; FlexJD students often don't receive fall grades until January–February, creating a structural timing gap. Start outreach and relationship-building before grades are even a factor — it's the one lever available in the meantime. LinkedIn alumni outreach: Search NUSL graduates by practice area keyword (mergers and acquisitions, venture capital, entrepreneurship, in-house counsel, securities). Send a short message asking for 10 minutes of Zoom career advice — not a job. Alumni respond to this. See the LinkedIn Alumni Outreach Guide in the All-Purpose Guide for step-by-step instructions. Law Review writing competition: Confirm FlexJD eligibility and submission timing with career placement now, not in September. Big law strategy: Meet with Hanna Kim before fall semester begins.Hanna Kim — NUSL Career Placement
2LsSummer associate applications are time-sensitive. If targeting big law for Summer 2027, applications may already be open — verify OCI dates immediately. Map the OCI application window against the FlexJD academic calendar — it does not automatically align. Co-op Spring 2027 cycle planning should begin this summer.Hanna Kim — NUSL Career Placement
3LsSummer 2028 positioning and bar jurisdiction planning (cross-reference the bar exam section of this newsletter). FlexJD graduation timeline creates unique OCI considerations — firm-specific flexibility varies significantly. Consult Hanna Kim on which firms have accommodated FlexJD timelines before applying.Hanna Kim — NUSL Career Placement

Cross-cohort: if you're interested in transactional, corporate, or securities law, take Corporations as soon as you're eligible — don't wait. Separately, the big law DEI recruiting landscape has shifted materially over the past year; talk to career advising for current, practice-specific guidance rather than relying on last year's assumptions.
