# Source Verification and Freshness Policy

## Purpose

This policy governs institutional information, academic requirements, contacts, deadlines, opportunity listings, campaign resources, and other factual material published through the FlexJD SBA Resource Hub.

## Source hierarchy

Use sources in this order whenever available:

1. official Northeastern University or Northeastern University School of Law webpages, catalogs, policies, offices, and written communications intended for the relevant audience;
2. official employer, court, agency, nonprofit, fellowship, competition, or program webpages;
3. official application portals or current program documents;
4. reliable secondary sources only when no authoritative source exists, with the secondary status clearly disclosed.

Social posts, reposted listings, screenshots, newsletters, aggregators, and third-party summaries are leads, not final authority.

## Required record elements

For every material policy, deadline, requirement, contact, or opportunity, capture where technically feasible:

- title;
- authoritative source URL;
- source organization;
- date first added;
- date last verified;
- deadline or review date;
- status: active, rolling, expired, superseded, archived, or unverifiable;
- reviewer initials or identifier;
- concise notes explaining ambiguity or conflict.

## Verification rules

A record is verified only when a reviewer has opened the authoritative source and confirmed the published statement against it. Search-result snippets, AI-generated summaries, or copied descriptions do not count as verification.

When sources conflict:

- prefer the controlling official source;
- preserve the conflict in review notes;
- avoid publishing a definitive statement until the conflict is resolved;
- direct students to the responsible office or program administrator.

## Freshness schedule

- Fixed-deadline opportunities: review at least every 14 days while open.
- Rolling opportunities: review at least every 60 days.
- Institutional requirements and policies: review each academic term and after any announced policy change.
- Contact information: review each academic term.
- Campaign resources and external links: review annually and before republication.

## Expiration and archival

Expired items should not silently remain represented as open. Mark them expired, move them to an archive, or remove them. Preserve historically useful records only when their status and original cycle are unambiguous.

## Corrections

Corrections must cite an authoritative source. Material institutional corrections should be logged in the changelog or commit history with a description of what changed and why.

## Prohibited practices

Do not:

- invent missing deadlines or eligibility rules;
- treat AI output as a source;
- copy an entire third-party posting when a concise summary and source link will do;
- publish private correspondence without authorization;
- imply Northeastern endorsement of an external opportunity;
- retain personal information merely because it appeared in a prior public posting.

## Human review

Automated link and stale-date checks are triage tools. A passing workflow does not establish substantive accuracy, current eligibility, institutional approval, or legal permission to republish content.
