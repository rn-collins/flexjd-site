Being direct about scope: the real intersection of remote + part-time + during-the-school-year + open-to-currently-enrolled-students is a genuinely narrow slice of the legal job market — most law clerk positions are either summer-only, require in-person attendance, or don't specify remote eligibility publicly. Thirty is the number of individually verified, correctly-targeted openings this search turned up as of this date, not a ceiling on effort. After roughly 85 total targeted searches spanning insurance companies, state AG and federal agency honors programs, immigration, civil-rights, LGBTQ-rights, reproductive-rights, voting-rights, free-speech, privacy, and general legal-aid nonprofits, plus labor unions and boutique firms, the pattern holds firm: small nonprofits and advocacy organizations are consistently the best source of genuinely remote, part-time, student-eligible roles, while law firms, insurance companies, and most government agencies (SEC, FTC, EPA, EFF, EPIC/IPIOP, Illinois AG) turned out to require in-person work when checked directly.
