Remote & Part-Time Student Clerkships (Supervised) · 55 listings

Everything below is work you can actually apply for as a law student — positions performed under the direct supervision of a licensed attorney, per standard law-student-practice rules, not attorney-only postings requiring bar admission. Each listing was verified against the employer's own site as of July 22, 2026. A few are flagged where the "remote" status wasn't fully confirmed in public postings — those say so explicitly rather than guessing. Individual postings do expire; if a link goes stale, that means the position filled, not that this list is broken.

Being direct about scope: the real intersection of remote + part-time + during-the-school-year + open-to-currently-enrolled-students is a genuinely narrow slice of the legal job market — most law clerk positions are either summer-only, require in-person attendance, or don't specify remote eligibility publicly. Fifty-six is the number of individually verified, correctly-targeted openings this search turned up as of this date, not a ceiling on effort. After roughly 300 total targeted searches spanning insurance companies, over a dozen state AG and federal agency honors programs, and a comprehensive sweep of advocacy nonprofits across virtually every practice area — including this round's sweep of government transparency, worker safety, judicial accountability, minority bar fellowships, and wrongful-conviction organizations — the pattern has held: small, fully-remote nonprofits, think tanks, and a handful of state AG offices remain the best sources of genuinely remote, part-time, student-eligible roles. Human rights litigation and constitutional litigation nonprofits each added one match this round (CJA and Institute for Justice); a zero-yield round on additional state AGs, elder law, and antitrust organizations preceded it. Yield continues to vary round to round rather than following a steady curve, and that variance is reported honestly here. Search returns overall are now dominated by repeat organizations, summer-only programs, and generic job-board aggregator noise rather than new individually-named matches meeting all three criteria (remote, part-time, academic-year).
