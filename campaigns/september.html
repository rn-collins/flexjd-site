One campaign, two months, one framing

September is Suicide Prevention Awareness Month. October is Domestic Violence Awareness Month. This hub treats them as one integrated campaign — because survivorship and crisis risk are documented, inseparable realities, and the resources that serve one serve the other.

Safe messaging is non-negotiable. Read the safe-messaging section before publishing anything — it governs language, imagery, and how crisis lines appear on every post, flyer, event, and email.

Why these two months connect

Survivors of sexual assault are 10x more likely to attempt suicide than those who haven't experienced SA
NSVRC — nsvrc.org/blog_post/what-puts-survivors-increased-risk-suicide-and-how-help/
Survivors whose first assault occurred before age 16 face 3–4x higher suicide attempt risk
NSVRC (same article)
23% of DV survivors have attempted suicide vs. 3% in non-exposed populations
DomesticShelters.org — domesticshelters.org/articles/health/domestic-violence-survivors-at-higher-risk-for-suicide
Women with IPV history: 7x more likely to report current suicidal ideation after controlling for sociodemographic variables and childhood abuse
NIH/PMC — pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3152586/
PTSD and trauma are associated with elevated suicide risk; ~26.6% of 2023 veteran suicides involved PTSD
va.gov/understand/related/suicide_ptsd.asp
VA National Center for PTSD — ptsd.
11% of law students had suicidal thoughts in the past year (up from 6% in 2014)
2021 Survey of Law Student Well-Being (5,400 students, 39 law schools)
80%+ of law students reported trauma in at least 1 of 15 categories
Same survey
Lawyers rank 5th in suicide by occupation; ~28% experience depression
Dave Nee Foundation
NCDVTMH — 'Recommendations for Suicide Prevention Hotlines on Responding to IPV'
ncdvtmh.org/resources/suicide/

Safe-messaging guidelines

Crisis resources
Always include 988 AND Find@Northeastern on every post, flyer, and event
Method/means
Focus on warning signs, how to help, and resources
Statistics
Cite stats with named sources and pair immediately with a resource
AFSP 'How to Talk Safely About Suicide'
afsp.org/how-to-talk-safely-about-suicide/
Action Alliance Framework for Successful Messaging
suicidepreventionmessaging.org
Before the event
Disclose WHO in the room is a mandatory reporter BEFORE any discussion begins; let participants opt out of sharing. Invite survivors to draft moderate questions and consent to any visibility. Provide schedule, breaks, restroom info. Ensure accessibility (captions, ASL if needed).
During
Do NOT put survivors on the spot. Monitor participant distress; have an OPEN UHCS BARCC contact available. Keep discussion at a systems advocacy level; redirect if it becomes clinical.
After
Post resources in event follow-up email. Check in with student volunteers. Flag any disclosures to OPEN for follow-up support.
NSVRC Trauma-Informed Care hub
nsvrc.org/trauma-informed-care
NFPCSA 'Planning a Trauma-Informed Event'
nfpcsa.pubpub.org/pub/fzej0m8t
